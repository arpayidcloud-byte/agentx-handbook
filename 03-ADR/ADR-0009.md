# ADR-0009: Contract-Test-Template Required Before Volume Approval
**Status:** Final | **Implements:** RFC-0018 | **Volume:** 14

## Context
Constitution Principle 6 (Testable by Default) needs a concrete, checkable definition to
be enforceable rather than aspirational.

## Decision
Volume approval has two distinct states:

- **Approved — Architecture:** The Volume's specification content (objectives, scope,
  chapters, interfaces, functional requirements) is finalized and accepted by the Project
  Owner. This state does not require a contract-test template.
- **Approved — Implementation-Gated:** In addition to Approved — Architecture, a
  corresponding contract-test template exists at
  `08-Examples/<volume-slug>/contract.test.ts`, covering at minimum the Volume's stated
  Functional Requirements and documented edge cases. No Volume may enter this state
  without that file.

All 16 Volumes in the v1.0 handbook currently hold **Approved — Architecture** status.
None currently hold Approved — Implementation-Gated status, because `08-Examples/` is not
yet populated for any Volume (see `08-Examples/README.md`).

## Consequences
- Every Volume's Acceptance Criteria implicitly gains one more checklist item before it
  can move from Approved — Architecture to Approved — Implementation-Gated.
- `08-Examples/` becomes populated incrementally as each Volume moves toward
  Implementation-Gated status, not left empty as in the original skeleton ZIP.
- Engineering work may begin against Approved — Architecture Volumes; a Volume reaching
  Implementation-Gated status confirms its contract is test-verified, not that
  implementation is blocked until then.
