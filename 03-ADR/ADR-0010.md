# ADR-0010: 14-Volume Handbook Structure
**Status:** Superseded (by the 16-Volume structure — see below) | **Volume:** 1 (Foundation), supersedes the original 12-Volume-only scope

> **Superseded 2026-07-20.** This ADR recorded the extension from 12 to 14 Volumes. The
> handbook has since grown to **16 Volumes** with the addition of Volume 15 (Identity &
> Access Foundation) and Volume 16 (Secrets & Key Management), introduced by ADR-0012,
> ADR-0015, and ADR-0016. The "14-Volume" count and any "all cross-references use the
> 14-Volume map" claim below are **no longer accurate**: the canonical count is **16**.
> This ADR is retained for historical record; per CONTRIBUTING.md a Final ADR is never
> edited to reverse itself, so this supersession note is additive and the 12→14 decision
> itself still stands as one step in the 12→14→16 progression.

## Context
Volume 1's authoring process identified two cross-cutting concerns — observability and
testing/QA methodology — substantial enough to deserve their own Volumes rather than
being bolted onto Core Runtime or the Constitution as subsections.

## Decision
Extend the original 12-Volume map with Volume 13 (Observability & SRE) and Volume 14
(Testing & QA Strategy), numbered sequentially at the end so the original 1–12 dependency
table (Volume 1, Ch. 3) remains unchanged.

## Consequences
- Handbook is now 14 Volumes; all cross-references in this delivery use the 14-Volume map.
  *(Superseded: the handbook is now 16 Volumes — see the supersession note above.)*
- Constitution Principle 6 (Testable by Default) now has a concrete home (Volume 14)
  instead of being enforced only by convention.
